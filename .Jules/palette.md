## 2024-11-28 - Icon-Only Buttons Lacking Accessibility Labels
**Learning:** In Vanilla JS applications, it's common to find native `<button>` elements that only contain emoji characters (like ✕ for close, or 🗑️ for delete) to serve as icon-only controls. Without explicit `aria-label` and `title` attributes, these buttons are inaccessible to screen reader users, who will just hear the emoji name (or nothing at all), lacking the context of the button's action.
**Action:** When adding or modifying icon-only buttons (especially those using emojis), always ensure they have an `aria-label` attribute describing their action (localized to the app's language, e.g., Arabic) and a `title` attribute for a visual tooltip on hover. This pattern must be actively checked during UX/a11y reviews.
